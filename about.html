SONA is a magazine dedicated to experimentation in music and other sound practices. We provide a space for listening and creation for projects that might not find a place in other contexts. Our focus is on works that embody freedom, deviation, or experimentation, whether in language, intention, or production, highlighting artists who challenge conventions and explore new ways of listening, creating, and engaging with sound.

Based in Brazil, the magazine offers a careful follow-up of contemporary sonic trajectories. We publish interviews, essays, reviews, and exclusive content, giving visibility to practices that cross boundaries between technique, improvisation, and perception, connecting body, space, and technology in investigative creative processes.

SONA is also a space for dialogue and reflection. Through critical texts and exclusive releases, we aim to create experiences of active listening, where music and sound art are not merely consumed but lived, felt, and perceived as agents of transformation and inventive possibilities for relating to the world.
